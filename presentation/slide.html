Data Layout Beats Parallelism:
SoA Outperforms OpenMP AoS by 5–8× on Numeric Queries

CMPE-273 Mini 1  |  NYC 311 Service Requests 2020–2024 (20M records, 12 GB)  |  Anu Myadala

The Finding

~8×
faster geographic bounding-box queries
in Phase 3 (SoA) vs Phase 1 (Serial AoS)

A geo-box query over 20 million records only needs latitude and longitude — 16 bytes per record. In Array-of-Structures (AoS), each record is ~500 bytes wide: the CPU must load and discard 97% of every cache line to reach those 16 bytes.

Structure-of-Arrays (SoA) stores all latitudes together and all longitudes together. The same query now reads two tight double[] streams that fit in L3 cache — and GCC 13 auto-vectorises them with 256-bit AVX2 SIMD instructions.

Why this matters beyond this assignment: Urban analytics systems — real-time 311 dashboards, GIS services, emergency dispatch — run geo-filter queries continuously. An 8× latency reduction means the difference between a 40 ms interactive response and a 5 ms one on live city data.
